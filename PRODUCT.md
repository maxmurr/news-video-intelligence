# Product

Broadcast Desk is AI Q&A over news video with timestamps you can trust. This page records register, users, purpose, positioning, and design principles.

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Analysts and researchers extracting facts from TV news. They face 30 to 60 minute broadcasts they cannot afford to watch end-to-end, and need to move from overview to specific claims without losing the thread.

## Product purpose

Broadcast Desk turns a news video into an interactive research surface: browse the main stories as a readable edition, then ask follow-up questions whose answers stay grounded in the source footage.

Success means you:

- leave with a clear answer and can point to the exact moment in the video
- finish a research pass in a fraction of the broadcast runtime
- trust the tool enough that hand-scrubbing the timeline becomes rare

## Positioning

AI Q&A over news video with timestamps you can trust.

## Brand personality

Sharp, fast, investigative. Voice is direct and evidence-first: no fluff, no confidence theater. Pace and density take cues from Linear: information dense, responsive, and free of decorative chrome.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI chat dashboards (purple glow, soft blobs, empty “Ask anything” states)
- Broadsheet / newspaper pastiche (dense columns, hairline rules, “edition” cosplay)
- Consumer video apps (YouTube-style theater, recommendations, entertainment chrome)

## Design principles

1. **Proof before polish**: every claim should reach footage.
2. **Timestamps earn trust**: answers are credible because they cite moments, not because they sound confident.
3. **Speed without superficiality**: dense and scannable; never empty or performative.
4. **Research desk, not entertainment player**: the UI serves verification and extraction, not watching as leisure.

## Accessibility and inclusion

Aim for WCAG 2.2 AA: sufficient contrast, full keyboard access, visible focus, and respectful reduced-motion alternatives.
